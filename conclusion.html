Conclusion

What We Learned

March 14th, 2019

The 2018 Eagles were eliminated in the second round of the playoffs. They struggled most of the year and their playoff chances were dependent on the last week of the season. After getting into the playoffs they defeated the Chicago Bears. During the Super Bowl run in 2017, the Eagles were significantly better in every major category on both sides of the ball. The significant improvement in passing yards from 2017 to 2018 is misleading because on the surface it appears as an improvement. In reality, the 2018 Eagles were playing from behind in most of their games causing them to throw the ball more.

Nick Foles and Carson Wentz shared a season once again prompting a quarterback controversy. Some experts believed that Nick Foles was deserving of the starting job over Wentz. We were able to see that Nick Foles in any given game is about as good as Wentz at his worst. When it came down to pay Foles, the Eagles declined and the Jacksonville Jaguars signed Foles to a 4 year, $88 million contract.

The Eagles take the running back by committee approach which means they use multiple running backs instead of one main workhorse. This approach served the Eagles well in the 2017 season, but the Eagles lost key running backs to free agency and injuries including LaGarrette Blount, Jay Ajayi and Darren Sproles. Their replacements failed to fulfill the role.

The additions of Desean Jackson and L.J. Fort should improve the Eagles on both sides of the ball. The Eagles future looks optimistic as they move forward into the 2019 season.
